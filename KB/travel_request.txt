BUSINESS TRAVEL REQUEST AND REIMBURSEMENT PROCEDURES

OVERVIEW
All business travel must be approved in advance and comply with company travel policies. This guide covers the complete process from request submission to expense reimbursement.

TRAVEL REQUEST SUBMISSION PROCESS

Step 1: Pre-Approval Planning
Before submitting a travel request, ensure you have:
- Clear business purpose and objectives
- Proposed travel dates and duration
- Estimated budget (transportation, accommodation, meals, incidentals)
- Manager approval in principle
- Meeting agendas, conference details, or client invitations (if applicable)

Step 2: Submit Travel Request
1. Log into the Operations Portal at https://ops.techcorp.com
2. Navigate to "Travel" > "New Travel Request"
3. Complete all required fields:
   - Traveler Information: Name, employee ID, department
   - Trip Details:
     * Purpose of travel (conference, client meeting, training, etc.)
     * Destination(s) and dates
     * Departure and return times
     * Number of travelers
   - Estimated Expenses:
     * Airfare/train/bus tickets
     * Hotel accommodations
     * Ground transportation (rental car, taxis, rideshare)
     * Meals and per diem
     * Conference fees or registration costs
     * Miscellaneous expenses
   - Business Justification: Detailed explanation of business need
   - Supporting Documents: Attach invitations, agendas, or meeting confirmations

4. Select Approval Workflow:
   - Manager approval (required for all requests)
   - Finance approval (required for expenses over $1,000)
   - Executive approval (required for expenses over $5,000 or international travel)

5. Submit request and await approval notifications

APPROVAL TIMELINES AND DEADLINES
- Standard requests: Submit at least 2 weeks in advance
- Domestic travel: Minimum 1 week notice required
- International travel: Minimum 3 weeks notice required (additional documentation may be required)
- Emergency travel: Contact your manager and finance department immediately
- Late submissions: May not be approved or may incur higher costs

TRAVEL BOOKING PROCESS

Once Approved:
1. You'll receive email confirmation with approval details
2. Travel Coordinator will contact you within 2 business days
3. Coordinate booking preferences:
   - Preferred airlines and flight times
   - Hotel preferences and location requirements
   - Ground transportation needs
   - Special accommodations or dietary requirements

Company-Preferred Vendors:
- Airlines: Corporate accounts with major carriers (discounted rates)
- Hotels: Preferred hotel chains with negotiated rates
- Car Rentals: Enterprise, Hertz, or Avis corporate accounts
- Travel Agencies: Corporate travel management company handles bookings

Self-Booking (If Authorized):
- Use company-approved booking platforms
- Follow per diem and accommodation guidelines
- Save all receipts for reimbursement
- Submit booking confirmation to travel coordinator

TRAVEL EXPENSE POLICIES

Per Diem Rates (Daily Allowances):
- Domestic Travel: $75/day for meals and incidentals
- International Travel: Varies by country (see IT Portal for rates)
- Hotel: Company-approved hotels within reasonable rates for destination
- Transportation: Economy class for flights under 4 hours, business class for longer flights

Reimbursable Expenses:
- Airfare, train, or bus tickets
- Hotel accommodations (single occupancy standard)
- Ground transportation (taxis, rideshare, rental cars, parking)
- Meals (within per diem limits)
- Business-related phone calls and internet
- Conference fees and registration
- Tips and gratuities (within reasonable limits)

Non-Reimbursable Expenses:
- Personal entertainment and activities
- Alcohol (unless part of business meal)
- Personal shopping or gifts
- Spouse or family travel expenses
- Travel insurance (covered by company policy)
- Expenses exceeding per diem without justification

EXPENSE REPORTING AND REIMBURSEMENT

Step 1: Collect Documentation
During your trip, keep all receipts for:
- Transportation (boarding passes, tickets, receipts)
- Accommodation (hotel invoices)
- Meals (itemized receipts)
- Other business expenses (receipts required for expenses over $25)

Step 2: Submit Expense Report
1. Log into Operations Portal within 5 business days of return
2. Navigate to "Travel" > "Expense Reports" > "New Report"
3. Enter trip details and attach all receipts
4. Categorize expenses appropriately
5. Add notes for any unusual expenses
6. Submit for manager approval

Step 3: Reimbursement Processing
- Manager reviews and approves expense report
- Finance processes payment
- Reimbursement typically received within 10 business days
- Payment method: Direct deposit to your bank account on file

INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL REQUIREMENTS
- Valid passport (check expiration date - must be valid 6 months beyond travel dates)
- Business visas if required (HR assists with visa applications)
- Travel insurance (automatically provided by company)
- Health and safety briefings (required for certain destinations)
- Register with embassy/consulate if traveling to high-risk areas
- Review country-specific travel advisories

TRAVEL SAFETY AND SECURITY
- Register your travel itinerary with security@techcorp.com
- Keep emergency contact information accessible
- Use company-approved travel booking services
- Follow local laws and customs
- Report any security incidents immediately
- Use secure Wi-Fi or VPN when accessing company systems

CANCELLATION AND CHANGES
- Changes to approved travel: Submit modification request through portal
- Cancellations: Notify travel coordinator and manager immediately
- Cancellation fees: Company covers reasonable cancellation costs
- No-show penalties: Employee responsible unless emergency situation

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Can I extend my trip for personal time?
A: Yes, but personal days are at your expense. Book return flight separately for personal extension.

Q: Can I use personal credit card for travel?
A: Yes, but company credit cards are available for frequent travelers. Contact finance for card application.

Q: What if my expenses exceed the estimate?
A: Submit justification for additional expenses. Approval depends on business necessity and reasonableness.

Q: Can I combine business and personal travel?
A: Yes, but only business portion is reimbursable. Clearly separate business and personal expenses.

Q: What if I need to cancel my trip?
A: Notify travel coordinator immediately. Company covers cancellation fees for business reasons.
