EQUIPMENT CHECKOUT AND ASSET MANAGEMENT

OVERVIEW
TechCorp maintains a comprehensive equipment inventory for employee use. This guide covers the process for checking out company equipment, usage policies, and return procedures.

AVAILABLE EQUIPMENT FOR CHECKOUT

Technology Equipment:
- Laptops and tablets (for temporary use, travel, or special projects)
- Monitors and displays (for remote work setups)
- Projectors and presentation equipment
- Audio/video equipment (cameras, microphones, speakers)
- Network equipment (routers, switches for testing)
- Mobile devices (smartphones, tablets for field work)

Office Equipment:
- Printers and scanners
- Conference room equipment
- Whiteboards and presentation boards
- Office furniture (desks, chairs for temporary setups)

Specialized Equipment:
- Development hardware (test devices, development boards)
- Testing equipment (multimeters, oscilloscopes)
- Field equipment (portable workstations, rugged devices)

CHECKOUT PROCESS

Step 1: Determine Equipment Needs
Before requesting equipment:
- Identify specific equipment needed
- Determine duration of use
- Check equipment availability in portal
- Verify you have necessary training/certifications (for specialized equipment)

Step 2: Submit Checkout Request
1. Log into Operations Portal at https://ops.techcorp.com
2. Navigate to "Equipment" > "Equipment Checkout"
3. Complete checkout request form:
   - Select equipment category and specific item
   - Enter checkout date and expected return date
   - Specify intended use and business justification
   - Note any special requirements or accessories needed
   - Provide project code or cost center if applicable
4. Submit for manager approval

Step 3: Approval Process
- Manager reviews and approves request
- IT/Operations verifies equipment availability
- You receive email confirmation with:
  * Approved checkout dates
  * Pickup location and instructions
  * Equipment condition checklist
  * Return procedures

Step 4: Equipment Pickup
1. Arrive at designated pickup location (IT Equipment Room or Operations)
2. Present employee ID and checkout confirmation
3. Staff will:
   - Retrieve equipment from inventory
   - Conduct equipment inspection with you
   - Document any existing damage or issues
   - Provide user manuals and setup instructions
4. Sign equipment checkout form acknowledging:
   - Equipment condition
   - Return date
   - Responsibility for care and return
   - Liability for damage or loss

EQUIPMENT USAGE POLICIES

Usage Guidelines:
- Equipment is for business use only
- Personal use is prohibited
- Equipment must be used in accordance with company policies
- Follow manufacturer's usage guidelines
- Report malfunctions immediately

Care and Maintenance:
- Handle equipment with care
- Keep equipment in secure location when not in use
- Protect from damage, theft, or loss
- Do not modify or tamper with equipment
- Keep equipment clean and in good condition

Security Requirements:
- Use company-approved security software
- Enable encryption and password protection
- Do not store sensitive data without encryption
- Follow data security policies
- Report lost or stolen equipment immediately

RETURN PROCEDURES

Step 1: Prepare Equipment for Return
Before returning:
- Back up any personal data (equipment will be wiped)
- Remove personal files and applications
- Clean equipment (keyboard, screen, casing)
- Gather all accessories and cables
- Pack in original packaging if available

Step 2: Schedule Return
1. Log into Operations Portal
2. Navigate to "Equipment" > "My Checkouts"
3. Select equipment to return
4. Click "Schedule Return"
5. Select return date and time
6. Confirm return appointment

Step 3: Return Equipment
1. Arrive at designated return location at scheduled time
2. Present equipment and accessories
3. Staff will:
   - Inspect equipment for damage
   - Verify all accessories are included
   - Test equipment functionality
   - Document condition
4. Sign return form acknowledging return

EQUIPMENT DAMAGE AND LIABILITY

Damage Assessment:
- Normal wear and tear: No charge
- Accidental damage: May incur repair or replacement cost
- Negligence or misuse: Employee responsible for full cost
- Loss or theft: Employee responsible for replacement cost

Reporting Damage:
1. Report immediately to IT/Operations
2. Do not attempt repairs yourself
3. Provide details of incident
4. Work with staff to assess damage and costs
5. Review insurance coverage if applicable

EXTENSIONS AND RENEWALS

Requesting Extensions:
- Submit extension request at least 3 days before return date
- Provide justification for extended use
- Manager approval required
- Subject to equipment availability
- Cannot extend beyond maximum checkout period (typically 90 days)

RENEWAL PROCESS
- If equipment is needed long-term, discuss permanent assignment with manager
- May require separate procurement process
- IT/Operations can assist with permanent equipment requests

EQUIPMENT INVENTORY AND AVAILABILITY
- Check real-time availability in Operations Portal
- Popular items may have waiting lists
- Reserve equipment in advance for important projects
- Contact IT/Operations for availability questions

SPECIALIZED EQUIPMENT REQUIREMENTS
Some equipment requires:
- Training or certification
- Security clearance
- Special handling procedures
- Insurance coverage

Check with IT/Operations before requesting specialized equipment.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: How long can I keep equipment?
A: Standard checkout period is 30 days, extendable to 90 days maximum with approval.

Q: What if I damage the equipment?
A: Report immediately. Assessment will determine if repair/replacement cost applies.

Q: Can I take equipment home?
A: Yes, with manager approval and for business purposes. Follow security guidelines.

Q: What if equipment malfunctions?
A: Contact IT Helpdesk immediately. Do not attempt repairs. Replacement may be provided.

Q: Can I check out equipment for personal use?
A: No, company equipment is for business use only.

Q: What happens if I lose equipment?
A: Report immediately to IT/Operations and Security. You may be responsible for replacement cost.

BEST PRACTICES
- Request equipment well in advance
- Return equipment on time or request extensions early
- Handle equipment with care
- Keep equipment secure
- Report issues immediately
- Follow all usage policies
