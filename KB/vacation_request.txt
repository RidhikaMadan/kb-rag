VACATION LEAVE REQUEST AND TIME-OFF MANAGEMENT

OVERVIEW
TechCorp recognizes the importance of work-life balance and encourages employees to take time off to recharge. This guide covers the complete process for requesting vacation leave, understanding accrual policies, and managing your time-off balance.

VACATION ACCRUAL POLICIES

Accrual Rates by Tenure:
- 0-1 years: 10 days (80 hours) per year, accrues at 0.0385 hours per hour worked
- 1-3 years: 15 days (120 hours) per year, accrues at 0.0577 hours per hour worked
- 3-5 years: 20 days (160 hours) per year, accrues at 0.0769 hours per hour worked
- 5-10 years: 25 days (200 hours) per year, accrues at 0.0962 hours per hour worked
- 10+ years: 30 days (240 hours) per year, accrues at 0.1154 hours per hour worked

Accrual Details:
- Vacation time accrues with each pay period
- Accrual begins on first day of employment
- Maximum carryover: 5 days (40 hours) can be carried to next year
- Unused vacation above carryover limit is forfeited on December 31
- Accrual stops when you reach the maximum balance

CHECKING YOUR VACATION BALANCE
1. Log into HR Portal at https://hr.techcorp.com
2. Navigate to "Time Off" > "Balance Summary"
3. View:
   - Accrued hours (earned but not used)
   - Available hours (can be used)
   - Pending requests (approved but not yet taken)
   - Scheduled time off (upcoming approved leave)

VACATION REQUEST PROCESS

Step 1: Plan Your Time Off
Before submitting a request:
- Check your available vacation balance
- Review team calendar for potential conflicts
- Identify critical project deadlines
- Plan coverage for your responsibilities
- Consider peak business periods (avoid if possible)

Step 2: Discuss with Your Manager
1. Schedule a brief conversation with your manager
2. Discuss:
   - Proposed dates and duration
   - Impact on current projects
   - Coverage arrangements
   - Any critical deadlines during your absence
3. Get preliminary approval before formal submission

Step 3: Submit Vacation Request
1. Log into HR Portal: https://hr.techcorp.com
2. Navigate to "Time Off" > "Request Time Off"
3. Complete the request form:
   - Select "Vacation" as leave type
   - Enter start date and end date
   - Specify number of days/hours requested
   - Add reason (optional but recommended)
   - Note coverage arrangements
   - Attach any supporting documents if needed
4. Review your request summary
5. Submit for manager approval

Step 4: Approval Process
- Manager receives notification within 24 hours
- Manager reviews and approves/denies within 3 business days
- You receive email notification of decision
- Approved requests appear in your time-off calendar
- Denied requests include reason for denial

ADVANCE NOTICE REQUIREMENTS
- Standard requests: Minimum 2 weeks advance notice
- Extended leave (5+ days): Minimum 4 weeks advance notice
- Peak periods (holidays, end of quarter): Minimum 4 weeks notice
- Emergency situations: Contact manager immediately, approval at manager's discretion

BLACKOUT PERIODS
Certain periods may have restricted vacation:
- End of fiscal quarters (last 2 weeks)
- Major product launches (as announced)
- Critical project deadlines (team-specific)
- Holiday periods (limited slots available)

Check with your manager or HR for current blackout periods.

COVERAGE AND DELEGATION
Before taking vacation:
1. Identify key responsibilities that need coverage
2. Delegate tasks to team members
3. Document procedures for coverage person
4. Set up out-of-office email and voicemail
5. Provide emergency contact information to manager
6. Complete or postpone urgent tasks

VACATION REQUEST APPROVAL CRITERIA
Managers consider:
- Available vacation balance
- Advance notice provided
- Team coverage and workload
- Business needs and deadlines
- Blackout periods
- Previous vacation patterns
- Fair distribution among team members

CANCELLING OR MODIFYING REQUESTS
- Before approval: Can cancel or modify in HR Portal
- After approval: Contact manager to discuss changes
- Last-minute cancellations: Notify manager and HR immediately
- Modifications: Submit new request, note it's a modification

EXTENDED LEAVE
For vacation requests exceeding 2 weeks:
- Requires additional approval from department head
- May require coverage plan documentation
- Consider impact on project timelines
- Discuss with HR for extended leave options

VACATION PAYOUT
- Upon termination: Unused accrued vacation paid out
- Payout calculated at current salary rate
- Received in final paycheck
- Subject to applicable taxes

HOLIDAYS AND VACATION
- Company holidays don't count against vacation balance
- If holiday falls during vacation, you're paid for holiday
- Plan vacation around holidays to maximize time off

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Can I take vacation during my probationary period?
A: Generally discouraged, but may be approved for exceptional circumstances. Discuss with manager.

Q: What if I'm sick during my vacation?
A: Vacation time is used. Sick leave cannot be substituted retroactively.

Q: Can I split my vacation into multiple shorter periods?
A: Yes, as long as you have available balance and provide proper notice.

Q: What happens if I don't use all my vacation?
A: Up to 5 days can carry over to next year. Balance above that is forfeited.

Q: Can I request vacation for the same day I submit?
A: Emergency requests may be approved at manager's discretion, but 2 weeks notice is standard.

Q: What if my manager denies my request?
A: Discuss reasons for denial. You can resubmit with adjusted dates or appeal to HR if you believe denial is unfair.

BEST PRACTICES
- Plan vacation well in advance
- Coordinate with team members to avoid coverage gaps
- Use vacation time regularly (don't let it accumulate unnecessarily)
- Check blackout periods before planning
- Set clear out-of-office messages
- Disconnect from work during vacation (unless emergency)
- Return refreshed and ready to contribute
